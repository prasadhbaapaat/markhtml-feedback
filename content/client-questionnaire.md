# Employee Onboarding System Discovery Questionnaire

This document is intended to gather the business, process, compliance, and technical requirements needed before planning an employee onboarding system for Texam Projects LLP. Texam publicly presents itself as a fully integrated engineering services contracting company with work across mechanical and process piping, fabrication, HT and LT electrical, PHE, HVAC, and instrumentation and controls, so the onboarding process may need to support both office staff and project or site-based employees.[cite:9][cite:3]

## 1. Business overview

1. What types of employees will use this onboarding process — office staff, engineers, supervisors, site staff, contract staff, trainees, or all of them?
2. Are there different onboarding processes for permanent employees, probationary employees, consultants, and project-based hires?
3. Which departments should be covered in phase 1?
4. Which locations or project sites should be included?
5. Is the system only for new hires, or should it also support transfers, promotions, and rejoining employees?
6. What is the current onboarding process today — manual paper-based, Excel, WhatsApp, email, or a mix?
7. What are the biggest problems in the current process — delays, missing documents, lack of tracking, repeated data entry, approval confusion, or printing issues?
## 2. Goals and success criteria

1. What do you want this system to improve first — speed, accuracy, employee experience, compliance, visibility, or reduced paperwork?
2. What would make this project a success after launch?
3. Approximately how many employees are onboarded per month?
4. Are there seasonal spikes in hiring or project-based bulk onboarding?
5. Do you want the system to reduce in-person visits before joining day?

## 3. User roles

1. Who will use the system internally — HR, directors, project managers, department heads, accounts, admin, IT, safety, security, or others?
2. What actions should each role be allowed to perform?
3. Who creates the onboarding record?
4. Who verifies employee documents?
5. Who gives final joining approval?
6. Should reporting managers complete any part of the onboarding checklist?
7. Should employees be able to see task status and pending approvals?

## 4. Employee categories

1. What employee categories exist — full-time, contractual, trainee, intern, consultant, site worker, vendor staff?
2. Does each category require a different form set or checklist?
3. Are some roles site-specific and others head-office-specific?
4. Are technical certifications or safety credentials mandatory for some categories?
5. Are different appointment letter templates needed by employee category?

## 5. Joining workflow

1. When should onboarding start — after interview selection, after offer acceptance, or only after joining is confirmed?
2. What are the main workflow stages today from selection to joining?
3. Should there be a status flow such as Draft, Invited, In Progress, Under Review, Approved, Joined, and Closed?
4. Can HR send a secure onboarding link to the employee?
5. Should employees be able to save and continue later?
6. Should the onboarding form work on mobile phones?
7. Should there be a deadline for employees to complete forms and upload documents?
8. Should reminders be sent automatically by email, SMS, or WhatsApp?

## 6. Employee information to collect

1. What personal details are mandatory — full name, photo, date of birth, gender, marital status, blood group, mobile number, email?
2. What address details are required — present address, permanent address, both?
3. What family or nominee details are required?
4. What emergency contact details are required?
5. What employment details are required — department, designation, reporting manager, location, date of joining, employee code, salary structure?
6. What payroll details are required — bank account, IFSC, UAN, ESIC, PAN, Aadhaar?
7. Are there any company-specific declarations or internal codes to capture?
8. Do different roles need additional fields, for example site assignment, discipline, project name, or safety category?

## 7. Documents to collect

1. Which documents are mandatory for every employee?
2. Which of the following are required: photo, Aadhaar, PAN, address proof, bank proof, educational certificates, experience letters, relieving letter, resume, passport, driving licence, UAN or PF details, ESIC documents?
3. Are there role-specific documents such as welding certificates, electrical licences, safety training certificates, client site passes, or medical fitness records?
4. What file types should be allowed — PDF, JPG, PNG?
5. What maximum upload size should be allowed?
6. Should multiple files be allowed for one document type?
7. Should HR be able to reject an uploaded document and ask for re-upload?
8. Should document expiry dates be tracked for licences or certifications?

## 8. Forms and printable documents

1. Which documents must the system generate automatically?
2. Which of these are needed: offer letter, appointment letter, NDA, code of conduct, joining form, employee information sheet, bank details form, emergency contact form, induction form, asset handover form, ID card request, PF or ESIC forms?
3. Which documents must be printable on A4 paper?
4. Which documents must also be downloadable as PDF?
5. Do any forms still need wet signatures after printing?
6. Do you want a full employee onboarding pack that can be printed in one click?
7. Should the system store a final signed copy after upload or scan?
8. Do you already have existing Word, PDF, or paper templates that must be followed?

## 9. Approvals and verification

1. Which steps require approval?
2. Who approves each step — HR, manager, accounts, admin, director?
3. Can approvals happen in parallel, or must they happen in a sequence?
4. What happens if a document is missing or incorrect?
5. Should approvers be able to leave comments?
6. Should the system maintain a full history of approvals and changes?
7. Should final joining be blocked until all mandatory approvals are complete?

## 10. Internal onboarding checklist

1. What tasks must HR complete?
2. What tasks must the reporting manager complete?
3. What tasks must accounts complete?
4. What tasks must admin or IT complete, for example email ID, laptop, SIM card, access card, software access?
5. Are there security, gate pass, or site access tasks?
6. Are there induction, safety, or compliance training tasks?
7. Should each task have an owner, due date, and completion status?
8. Should the employee be able to see only selected checklist items or the full process?

## 11. Compliance and policy needs

1. Which statutory compliance items must be covered during onboarding?
2. Are PF, ESIC, gratuity, or labour law declarations part of onboarding?
3. Are there internal policies that employees must acknowledge, such as confidentiality, code of conduct, leave policy, IT policy, safety policy, or anti-harassment policy?
4. Does the company need consent text for storing employee personal data?
5. Are there retention rules for old onboarding records?
6. Are any compliance requirements different for site staff versus office staff?

## 12. E-signature and signature process

1. Do you want electronic signatures, typed signatures, checkbox acknowledgment, or only printed signatures?
2. Which documents can be digitally accepted, and which must be physically signed?
3. Should the employee, HR, and manager all sign within the system?
4. Is legal validity of e-sign required, or is acknowledgment enough for phase 1?

## 13. Notifications and communication

1. How should communication happen — email, SMS, WhatsApp, phone call reminders?
2. Who should receive notifications at each stage?
3. Should employees receive a checklist summary and pending item reminders?
4. Should HR receive alerts for delayed or incomplete onboarding?
5. Should managers be notified when their action is pending?

## 14. Search, reports, and dashboards

1. What should HR see on the dashboard — total onboarding cases, pending documents, pending approvals, upcoming joining dates, overdue tasks?
2. What reports are required in phase 1?
3. Should users be able to filter by department, location, joining date, or status?
4. Is export to Excel or PDF required?
5. Should the system show how long each onboarding takes from invite to completion?

## 15. Access control and security

1. Which users should have full access and which users should have restricted access?
2. Should managers only see employees reporting to them?
3. Should salary-related data be hidden from some roles?
4. Should some documents be visible only to HR?
5. Is there a requirement for audit logs of every upload, edit, approval, and print action?
6. Should invite links expire automatically?
7. Do you want OTP login, password login, or both for employees?

## 16. Existing systems and integration

1. Is there already an HRMS, payroll software, attendance system, ERP, or biometric system in use?
2. Should this onboarding system integrate with any existing software?
3. Should employee data be exported to payroll after joining is complete?
4. Is there an existing employee code generation process?
5. Are there standard department, designation, and location masters already maintained elsewhere?

## 17. Branding and UX

1. Should the onboarding portal match the company website branding, logo, colors, and tone?
2. Should the portal be available in English only, or multiple languages?
3. Is mobile-first usability important for site or field employees?
4. Do you want a simple step-by-step wizard or a dashboard-style employee portal?
5. Should the employee experience be as short as possible, or is detailed data collection acceptable?

## 18. Implementation planning

1. What is the preferred timeline for phase 1?
2. Is there a target go-live date?
3. Who will be the main stakeholder for approvals and feedback?
4. Who will provide document samples and policy content?
5. Will there be one review round or multiple rounds before development starts?
6. Is there a fixed budget range for phase 1 and phase 2?
7. Do you prefer a minimum viable version first, followed by improvements?

## 19. Optional future features

1. Do you want employee self-service after onboarding, such as profile updates or document re-upload?
2. Should this later grow into a full HRMS?
3. Would you want modules like leave, attendance, payroll integration, asset management, or training management in the future?
4. Should the system later support offboarding as well?
5. Should the system later support contractor onboarding separately?

## 20. Final reference material to request from client

Please ask the client to share the following before planning starts:

- Current onboarding forms, checklists, and document formats. 
- Sample appointment letter, NDA, declaration, and policy documents.
- List of departments, roles, locations, and employee categories.
- Current approval flow and responsible persons.
- Mandatory document list by employee type.
- Statutory and compliance requirements.
- Branding assets such as logo and letterhead.
- Any existing Excel trackers or manual registers currently used.

## Discovery note

Texam’s website and hiring references indicate a business with engineering and project delivery operations, which is why the questionnaire includes site assignment, certification, compliance, and printable record requirements in addition to standard HR onboarding questions.[cite:9][cite:2][cite:3]
